Classic retro consoles
8×8 — earliest sprites (Atari, very old arcade)
16×16 — NES, early Game Boy
32×32 — SNES, Genesis
64×64 — larger RPG portraits, bosses, modern pixel-art “HD” style


Character sprites:
16×16 → “retro cute”
32×32 → “retro detailed”
64×64 → “modern pixel art, lots of detail”


Most famous pixel sizes
16×16 → Super Mario Bros., Zelda (NES), Final Fantasy I
32×32 → Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy VI
64×64 → Many modern indie pixel games (Stardew Valley uses ~16 but upscale)
